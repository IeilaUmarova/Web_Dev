In Germany

  1. Brandenburg Gate (Berlin): about it

    2. This iconic 18th-century monument symbolizes peace and unity. Located in the heart of Berlin, it is one of Germany's most recognizable landmarks.

  2. Neuschwanstein Castle (Bavaria): about it

    3. A fairy-tale castle that inspired Walt Disney's logo. Nestled in the picturesque Alps, it is one of Germany's most visited attractions.

  3. Cologne Cathedral (Cologne): about it

    4. A stunning Gothic cathedral and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its towering spires dominate the city's skyline and attract millions of visitors annually.

  4. The Black Forest (Baden-Württemberg): about it

    5. A magical region of dense forests and charming villages. Known for cuckoo clocks and hiking trails, it’s a must-see for nature lovers

  5. The Romantic Road: about it

    6. A scenic route passing through medieval towns, castles, and vineyards. bHighlights include Rothenburg ob der Tauber, a perfectly preserved medieval town.

In Italy

  1. Colosseum (Rome): about it

    2. This ancient amphitheater, built in 70–80 AD, is an iconic symbol of the Roman Empire and a masterpiece of engineering and architecture.

  2. Leaning Tower of Pisa (Pisa): about it

    3. A world-famous architectural wonder, the Leaning Tower is a stunning example of medieval engineering with its unique tilt.

  3. Venice Canals (Venice): about it

    4. Known as the "Floating City," Venice is famous for its enchanting canals and gondola rides. The Grand Canal is the city's main waterway, lined with historic buildings.

  4. Florence Cathedral (Florence): about it

    5. Also known as the Duomo, this Renaissance masterpiece is renowned for its magnificent dome designed by Filippo Brunelleschi.

  5. Amalfi Coast (Campania): about it

    6. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this stunning coastline is dotted with colorful villages, crystal-clear waters, and dramatic cliffs, making it one of the most picturesque spots in Italy.

In Spain

  1. Sagrada Família (Barcelona): about it

    2. Designed by Antoni Gaudí, this extraordinary basilica is a masterpiece of modernist architecture and one of the most iconic landmarks in Spain.

  2. Alhambra (Granada): about it

    3. A magnificent palace and fortress complex from the Moorish era, showcasing stunning Islamic art, architecture, and beautiful gardens.

  3. Park Güell (Barcelona): about it

    4. Another masterpiece by Antoni Gaudí, this whimsical park features colorful mosaics, unique sculptures, and breathtaking views of the city.

  4. Prado Museum (Madrid): about it

    5. One of the world’s finest art museums, it houses masterpieces by Spanish artists like Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco, along with works by other European greats.

  5. Seville Cathedral and La Giralda (Seville): about it

    6. A stunning Gothic cathedral and its adjacent bell tower, La Giralda, are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and showcase the city’s rich history and architecture.

In France

  1. Eiffel Tower (Paris): about it

    2. The iconic symbol of Paris and France, this iron tower offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks.

  2. Louvre Museum (Paris): about it

    3. The world’s largest art museum and home to famous masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.

  3. Palace of Versailles (Versailles): about it

    4. A magnificent royal residence known for its opulent architecture, stunning gardens, and the Hall of Mirrors.

  4. Mont Saint-Michel (Normandy): about it

    5. A picturesque island commune with a medieval abbey that rises dramatically from the sea, offering a magical atmosphere.

  5. French Riviera (Côte d'Azur): about it

    6. A glamorous Mediterranean coastline famous for its beautiful beaches, luxury resorts, and charming towns like Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez.
